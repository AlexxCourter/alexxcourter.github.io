Target Audience

The target audience of The Meteorology Station is both young and aspiring meteorologists and weather forecasters as well as seasoned professionals. The main feature that appeals to these two demographics of the audience is the learning environment. Professionals have the opportunity to contribute their expertise, therein building goodwill and rapport while supporting the next generation of meteorologists. Younger students and amateur meteorologists will be able to contribute with what they are learning, learn from professionals and peers about local weather patterns, and collaborate using the forums and other features.

Client personas

Persona 1: Chad Winters

Profile:

  • Age - 24
  • Income - $15,000/year
  • Employment - Part time grocery store associate
  • Education - Sophmore in college majoring in Meteorology
  • Experience with topic - Amateur meteorologist, academic level
  • Family status - Single
  • Device used - Mobile Phone and Tablet

Chad is an easygoing college student who wants to become a meteorologist. He has been studying weather patterns, and wants to improve his accuracy for his own forecasts. Chad learns about meteorologystation.com from a classmate who uses it. Excited he might be able to improve his skills through collaboration and seeing other people's perspective and opinion on the weather, he signs up for an account on his phone. Chad enjoys using the local weather section to practice his own forecasting and compare thoughts with his peers in the area.

Persona 2: Ryan Snoww

Profile:

  • Age - 38
  • Income - $90,000/year
  • Employment - Local News Meteorology Team
  • Education - Bachelors Degree related to Meteorology
  • Experience with topic - Professional Meteorologist, 10 years
  • Family status - Married

Ryan is a professional meteorologist who provides forecasts and current weather information to his local news station behind the scenes. Ryan saw meteorologystation.com in a targeted social media advertisement and decided to take a look. Ryan found the community aspect of meteorologystation.com appealing, and he started offering his expertise as a contributor to discussions about meteorology and local weather patterns. Through his participation, Ryan was able to make new connections in the industry who help him to be a better forecaster by collaborating in prediction theories and projects.

Scenarios

the following are example scenarios in which the site would be used.

  1. User wants to know what the weather forecast is in their town or other relevant information (primary function).
  2. User has a question about local weather patterns and wants to ask advice from peers or experts.
  3. User wants to connect with like-minded meteorologists by organizing/participating in in-person events or through virtual communication and networking.
  4. User wants to participate in a collaborative learning environment to improve their meteorology skills and knowledge - i.e. read and contribute to guides on forums, communicate with others about local weather patterns and information, seek collaboration on projects.
  5. An educator uses meteorologystation.com resources for his or her students, i.e. have students collaborate on a project in a group forum thread, use weather information, etc.
